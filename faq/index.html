Frequently asked questions

How does Super Greedy generate trade signals?

Super Greedy uses a proprietary AI model that analyzes price action, volume, and market structure in real time to generate high-probability entry and exit signals across configured instruments.

What markets does Super Greedy support?

Super Greedy is built for futures markets including ES, NQ, MES, and MNQ, with additional instruments configurable based on your trading setup.

Can I customize the strategy parameters?

Yes. Risk per trade, position sizing, daily loss limits, target levels, and timeframe preferences are all configurable so the strategy fits your trading style and account rules.

Does Super Greedy work with prop firm rules?

Yes. Super Greedy includes configurable risk parameters including daily loss limits, max drawdown thresholds, and position sizing controls that align with common prop firm evaluation rules including TopstepX.

How are supply and demand zones calculated?

Supply and demand zones are identified algorithmically based on price structure, momentum imbalances, and volume patterns — the same areas where institutional participants tend to transact. Zones refresh automatically as new data comes in.

Does Super Greedy execute trades automatically?

Yes. When automation is enabled, Super Greedy executes entries, manages stops, and closes positions based on your configured rules. You can also run it in signal-only mode and execute manually.

What is multi-timeframe analysis and how does it improve signals?

Multi-timeframe analysis checks that a lower-timeframe entry signal aligns with higher-timeframe price structure and bias. This filters out setups that look good on a 5-minute chart but are trading against a daily trend — resulting in fewer false signals and higher-quality entries.

What broker or platform does Super Greedy connect to?

Super Greedy connects to TopstepX and compatible futures brokers. See the features page for current integration details.

Can I use Super Greedy without automated execution?

Yes. Super Greedy can run in signal-only mode where it surfaces AI signals and zone data without placing trades automatically. This lets you review setups and execute manually at your discretion.

What happens if a signal results in a losing trade?

Losses are part of any trading strategy. Super Greedy manages this with configurable stop placements and daily loss limits that halt trading automatically when a threshold is reached — so one bad session doesn't blow the account.

How is Super Greedy different from trading manually?

Manual trading means reading charts, identifying zones, checking multiple timeframes, and executing — all in real time, under pressure. Super Greedy does all of that continuously and acts faster than a human can. The result is more consistent entries, less emotional decision-making, and a strategy that runs even when you're away from the screen.
